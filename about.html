About Japan Abduction Watch

A public watchdog run by and for families affected by parental child abduction in Japan.

Who We Are

Japan Abduction Watch (J.A.W.) is an independent, volunteer-run documentation project founded by left-behind parents. We are not affiliated with any government or political party. Our sole purpose is to create a permanent, verifiable public record of parental child abduction cases involving Japan — the record that no government agency currently keeps.

What We Publish, and How We Verify It

  • Cases are published only when supported by legal documents, court records, or public testimony.
  • Children's photos are published only with the consent of the child or their parent.
  • Court and police documents are archived as submitted, with personal data redaction encouraged before upload.
  • Every submission is manually reviewed before publication. Nothing goes public automatically.
  • Anyone who believes they are wrongly listed can request a formal review at any time — see our publication & privacy policy.

The Wider Movement

We stand alongside organizations that have documented and fought this crisis for years:

  • Bring Abducted Children Home (BACHome)
  • Kizuna Child-Parent Reunion
  • Find My Parent
  • Stop Japan Child Abduction

Key official documents: the European Parliament resolution of 8 July 2020, the U.S. State Department country page on Japan, and the Japanese Ministry of Foreign Affairs Hague Convention portal.
